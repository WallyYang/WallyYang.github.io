About Me

My name is Minrui Yang, and I go by Wally. Currently, I am a student at the Ohio State University major in Computer and Information Sciences and will graduate in May 2020 with Bachelor of Science as an honor student. I would like to make use of my programming and software developing experience to contribute codes as a software developer for users around the world.

I have been programming for about seven years with different platforms, programming languages, and frameworks with a strong software development basis and this makes me a experienced programmer even before my college study. I fell in love with programming since the first time I wrote a program. I love the idea to control a power machine like a laptop with simply words and phrases.

During the years when I studied programming independently and in college, I dealt with numerous kinds of software developing including both front-end and back-end for websites, GUI programming with Qt, UWP with C#, and server programming C, C++, and Java. The experience of designing, implementing, and refactoring software projects by myself helped me build a solid foundation of complicated software engineering projects.

My specialization in computer science is computer graphics, or CG. This came to my thoughts when I first install Arch Linux on my laptop and I found switching from a dark console to graphics like X-win or a window manager or a large desktop environment is very intriguing. Programmers like myself start their study in the console or “the dark window”, which is usually boring and frustrating. Therefore, I immediately fell into the passion to understand how a graphical window works when I wrote my first GUI program.
